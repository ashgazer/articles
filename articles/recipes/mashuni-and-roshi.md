# Mashuni & Roshi

Mashuni is a traditional Maldivian breakfast dish made from tuna, coconut, and spices. It's typically served with Roshi, a type of flatbread.

---

## 🥥 Mashuni

### Ingredients
- 200 g canned tuna, drained (about 2 small tins — preferably smoked or in oil)
- 90 g freshly grated coconut
- Fresh curry leaves 🍃, finely chopped
- 1 small red onion or a shallot, finely chopped
- 1 red chilli (or Scotch bonnet), finely chopped
- Juice of 1 lime
- Salt, to taste

### Instructions
1. In a bowl, combine the chopped onion, chilli, curry leaves, lime juice, and salt. Mix well, gently pressing the ingredients to release their flavours.
2. Add the drained tuna to the mixture and combine thoroughly.
3. Incorporate the grated coconut, mixing until evenly distributed.
4. Let the mixture sit for a few minutes to allow the flavours to meld.
5. Serve with freshly made Roshi.

> **Note:** For an authentic taste, use freshly grated coconut. If unavailable, rehydrate desiccated coconut in warm water or coconut milk for at least 10 minutes.

## 🫓 Roshi (Maldivian Flatbread)

Roshi is a soft, unleavened flatbread that complements Mashuni perfectly.

### Ingredients
- 300 g plain (all-purpose) flour, plus extra for dusting
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 teaspoon salt
- Approximately 200 ml warm water
- Optional: 50 g shredded coconut, for added flavour

### Instructions
1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and vegetable oil.
2. Gradually add the warm water, kneading until a smooth, non-sticky dough forms.
3. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
4. Divide the dough into golf ball-sized portions.
5. On a floured surface, roll each ball into a thin circle, approximately 15 cm in diameter.
6. Heat a skillet over medium heat. Cook each roshi for 1–2 minutes on each side, or until light brown spots appear.
7. Keep the cooked roshi covered with a clean towel to maintain softness.

> **Note:** Adding shredded coconut to the dough imparts a subtle sweetness and enhances the flavour.
